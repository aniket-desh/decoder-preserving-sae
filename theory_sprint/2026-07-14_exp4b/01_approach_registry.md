## Literature audit after the independent derivations

The closest prior work is [Harvey, Lipshutz, and Williams (2024)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.08197), which derives average decoding similarities and distances from distributions over linear tasks, including GULP as a choice of regularization. [Boix-Adserà et al. (2022)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.06545) introduce GULP as a prediction-based representation metric with uniform control for regularized linear tasks. Those papers own the broad connection between ridge prediction operators and representational similarity. This project should claim the use of that geometry as a sparse-autoencoder training objective and its matched experimental audit, not the underlying representation metric.

[Braun et al. (2024)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.12241) train end-to-end SAEs with output KL, and [Karvonen (2025)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.17272) shows that short KL+MSE fine-tuning recovers much of the downstream-loss improvement. They are the correct functional-preservation comparators, but their frozen output geometry is different from refittable ridge-task preservation. The zero-distance/frozen-output counterexample in this sprint makes that difference formal.

[Bhalla et al. (2026)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28119) analyze how SAEs capture concept manifolds, while [Wurgaft et al. (2026)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05115) connect activation-manifold paths to behavior through interventions. These papers make a curvature extension plausible, but Experiment 4b did not measure a manifold quantity and did not pass the frozen-behavior gate, so importing that theory now would weaken the paper's causal chain.

Targeted searches for `sparse autoencoder representational similarity regularizer`, `autoencoder preserve linear decodability`, and `decoder disagreement sparse autoencoder` did not surface a prior paper that trains an SAE directly on the finite-group Harvey/GULP ridge-hat discrepancy used here. That is evidence of an apparently open combination, not proof of novelty; the paper should say “to our knowledge” only after a broader citation-forward/backward search.

## Promotion rule

A result is promoted to `05_final_theory.md` only if it has a clear quantifier, a proof or reproducible check, and a statement of what Experiment 4b does or does not establish about it. Anything that depends on an unmeasured manifold, treats BatchTopK sparsity as matrix rank, or claims frozen-network control from a refitted decoder metric remains outside the core theory.
