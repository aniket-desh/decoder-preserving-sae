**External-evaluation status (July 15, 2026).** Standard concept discovery and a confirmatory frozen-network consequence evaluation are blocked pending collaborator compute. Exp08 already completed a three-seed frozen-model diagnostic on the once-sealed split, but it was designated review-only and did not predeclare a primary metric, noninferiority margin, or task-level outcome. Treat it as exploratory rather than as closure of the frozen-network gap.

The key correction is that isotropic decoder preservation does not identify semantically important low-variance directions under the rank-constrained relaxation. It has the same singular-direction ordering as MSE with a ridge-saturated distortion. Low-variance task selection is therefore tested separately using a structured prior, while the isotropic experiment studies spectral saturation under a genuinely sparse, nonorthogonal bottleneck.

The main-text evidence is now organized around the phenomenon rather than the original five-experiment chronology. The conceptual construction establishes that MSE and sparsity leave taskwise fidelity underdetermined; the rank relaxation explains the isotropic spectral limit; the structured task-prior generator tests task-selective sparse allocation; and the GPT-2 experiment tests held-out readout fidelity at matched reconstruction quality. Estimator audits, architecture checks, gradient diagnostics, and the failed IOI, open-ended semantic, and JumpReLU branches are controls or appendix evidence. Standard concept discovery remains the red external-evaluation TODO rather than an automatic extra headline.

The locked main-text empirical figures are now narrower than that original five-experiment outline. The structured-prior synthetic confirms protected-task selection: median held-out protected-task distortion falls by 25.3% versus paired MSE across 10/10 seeds without an observed NMSE penalty. The fresh GPT-2-small block-8 confirmation freezes $\gamma=0.03125$ on a disjoint selection range, then reduces exact decoder distortion by 10.61%, 11.38%, and 10.84% across three 100M-token paired seeds while matching or slightly improving NMSE. A one-seed tokenwise-TopK control retains exactly 32 activations per token and yields an 11.50% reduction at a 0.81% NMSE cost. These results establish protected linear-readout fidelity; they do not yet establish better standard concept discovery or confirmatory frozen-model behavior.

The next evaluation should reuse the existing matched MSE/DPSAE Pythia-160M-deduped block-8 residual-stream pair at width 16,384 and target $L_0=32$ before training anything new. It should compare concept probes on original activations, full reconstructions, and full sparse codes; run SAEBench `sae-probes` at $k\in\{1,2,5\}$; advance to three paired seeds at matched $L_0$ and NMSE only if the checkpoint-screen gap exceeds probe-reseed noise; and freeze feature descriptions before held-out scoring only for tasks whose advantage replicates. Automated descriptions are supporting evidence, while TPP and SCR are excluded as headline metrics because the 2026 reliability audit found that they fail multiple benchmark checks.

The completed JumpReLU validity study uses one-time first-batch quantile initialization, FP32 scores and threshold gradients, and rectangular-kernel pseudo-gradients. It trains the paired objectives for 25,001,984 tokens at shared sparsity-loss weights 2, 4, 8, and 16. The decoder-blind selector reads only held-out inference $L_0$, late-window drift, finite state, threshold health, dead-feature count, and run provenance. A weight can advance only if both $L_0$ confidence intervals lie in $[30.4,33.6]$, the paired mismatch is at most 1.6 activations, late drift is acceptable, and the maximum dead fraction is at most 10%.

No setting advances. The DPSAE/MSE $L_0$ pairs are $37.73/38.55$, $37.38/36.93$, $36.10/36.11$, and $35.88/35.61$ as the weight increases, and maximum dead fractions are 48.5--54.4%. NMSE, decoder distortion, and language-model loss remain sealed, so this is a controller-feasibility failure rather than a negative DPSAE effect estimate. We do not extend the grid after seeing the full-horizon result.

The single-seed GPT-2 block-4 screen passes its decoder-distortion gate, but the Pythia-160M-deduped block-8 screen reaches a 9.13% reduction and misses the frozen 10% gate. Both stay diagnostic rather than supporting a cross-layer or cross-model generality claim. Positive IOI, open-ended semantic-recurrence, and JumpReLU plots are likewise excluded from the current paper because their gates did not pass.
